"I don't understand," Rose shook her head. "ZERO has always been that way. And besides, he's part-Esper himself."

"But..." returner 2 stammered. "The way Terra was holding him close...it's like she's not really there at all...well, like they don't care about what's happening to this world anymore."

"What's happening to Baron and Dalmasca?" Rose asked.

"I'm not sure," returner 2 said. "But I did come across a man named Seraph who saw ZERO and Terra."

"Seraph," Rose echoed. "Wait! He was with us while we infiltrated Midgar. For a while, I wasn't really sure of his motives. What did he want? What was he really after?"

"To warn you, Strife," returner 2 said.

"Warn me of what?" Rose asked, confused.

"The Spirit Knights," returner 2 said. "Seraph believes Terra was using them in order to conquer the world and--"

"Whoa! Whoa! Wait just a damn minute!" Rose said. "Conquer? I don't believe that! ZERO would never fight for destruction! Using the Knights to free people from my mother's tyranny? Maybe. But using them to destroy everything? Just where is this going? I don't understand!"

"Strife--" returner 2 began before being interupted.

"ZERO would want to see this war end. That I know," Rose said. "And just think! No more greed. No more oppression. No more who lives and who dies. This world would be a better place!"

"That's excatly why Seraph needs your help, sir," returner 2 said. "He's already on his way to Dalmasca and it's only a matter of time before your mother and this Zane character make their move to conquer it."

"Zane," Rose echoed. "What the hell is he anyway? Some kind of war mongerer? And why does Seraph need my help! Look at us! We're only rebels! What have we to offer?"

"The people, sir," returner 1 speaks up. "That's what the Returners were originally about anyway, right?"

"But with ZERO gone," Rose said, "how would we be able to fight onwards. How would we get Alekssander home. I just...don't really know the way."

"Your compassion has helped us through," returner 2 suggested. "I mean, ZERO never really lived up to those qualities and you're the one who actually follows through our expectations."

Rose let out a deep sigh, wondering if ZERO would ever come back to finish what they have started. But what the fellow returner said is causing her worries within her heart. Was Terra trying to trick them all along. If so, what for? And will ZERO see the truth before it's too late?

"All right. I'm coming." Albert had been in his bedroom when someone started knocking at his door. He openned a door that let led to the back of his forge. This would have been where he conducted his business; he usually didn't invite people back. As he walked from back of the forge to the front door, he caught his toe on something. "Son of a...!" He grabbed his foot instinctively. When the pain quickly subsided, he looked down at what he had kicked. It was a black metal barbute helmet. He must have forgotten to pack it when he was sent to the army base. He reacked down, picked it up and examined it. The lip of the face hole ressembled wolf ears, a common symbol as it had been his family crest. It also had a saggital crest extending down to the back. Albert put the helm on a shelf where it should have been and saw a steel battle mask that would be used to protect his face, even it was designed to resemble the face of a snarling wolf. He stared at the two emblems for a while.

The knocking repeated. "What?!"

A man's voice on the other side of the door said, "Hello, I'm looking for a Mister Jazz. First name Hugh."

Albert smiled as he recognised the voice. "Look in a mirror." As he openned the door, a woman about his age hugged him, giving him a peck on the cheek. She let go of him and sat behind his desk of to the side, swinging her boots up on top of it. She wore black jeans and a black leather jacket. Her long red fly-aways trailed behind her. Under the jacket was a leather stap that resembled one from a messenger bag. Instead of a bag though, she had an Okatana at her hip.

"Nah, that's Jazzole." Albert turned to the man that was just coming in. They patted each other's shoulders. He wore a dark grey business suit. If he hadn't changed too much, he probably had two pistols stowed under his suit jacket. "Heard you been in town for a while, now." He walked to the side of the room and sat on the anvil. "Why haven't you visited your old friends yet?"

"Been thinking," Albert responded.

"What about?" inquired Carli, the red headed woman behind the desk.

After a short pause, Albert commented, "Nothin' really."

"Well that ain't much of an escuse, is it?" commented Noah, the man. He worked as a weapons designer for the army. That was why he used guns, even though most Noireans were swordsmen.

"Guess not. Want a drink?" Albert step towards the back to get some glasses.

"That isn't why we're here," commented Carli. She had now stood up and walked past him. When she entered his room before him, she began searching for something.

"We heard you'd been having a hell of a time lately. That's why the Acolytes sent us to get you. They've judged you worthy of Ascention." Noah stood and walked towards the front door.

"Here." As Albert turned, Carli tossed the Dancing Wolf Claw at him. Over her shoulder she also had his temple cloak.

"What, no catching up?" Albert said comicly.

"Nope. You missed you chance." Carli shoved him towards the door.

In a private room in the back of the temple, Albert knelt and prayed. He wasn't sure why the Acolytes had made this decision so suddenly. He only knew that they felt he deserved this power they were offering him. He openned his eyes, took up his sword and stepped to the door.

When he stepped into the main temple, Noah and Carli took places beside him. Both were Guardians, two tiers ahead of him. Their cloaks had silver shoulder padding and the hoods had a silver plating at the front. The escorted him to the center of the room. There, the Guardians turned around and stood sentry at the stairs to the pulpit. The Acolyte stood there already and took the sword and placed it on the alter. His hood was adorned with a large and ornate headdress. His face was hidden behind a silver mask. He raised his hands to begin the ceremony. His fellow Noireans stood and began chanting a mournful hymn. The sang for their brother to be gifted with strength, wisdom and rightousness. As the hymn continued, a thick mist billowed out of the torches around the temple. As the mist passed over his sword, a figure took shape. Albert watched it hover among the crowd. The Acolyte offered him a goblet from which to drink. It wasn't very full so Albert drained it. It was sour. As he lowered the cup, the figure in the mist lowered to the floor and took the form of a large wolf, he kept seeing them everywhere. The wolf stared at him for a while. The hymn ended and all the mist, the wolf included, vanished.

"See, that wasn't so bad." Noah had his arm around Albert's shoulder. The were walking toward the tavern. "Now, how 'bout those drinks?"

"Only if you're buying," joked Carli. Albert couldn't help but smile. She saw this and said, "It's about time you lightened up."

When they entered the tavern, the Barkeep greeted them, "Welcome, brothers and sister. What may I offer you?"

"What do you recommend for a celebration, brother?" asked Noah, taking a seat at a table. There were two other chairs: Albert took a seat with his back to the door; Carli sat to his left, putting feet on the table.

"I have a perfect Rose wine that just arrived from the vineyards of Kalm the other day. Sister, could you please keep your feet on the floor?" She did as he requested.

"That will be fine, brother," replied Noah. The barkeep went to his stockroom in the back.

For a while they traded jokes and stories about old times. Albert; however, remained distracted. The wolves that kept appearing before him seemed to have some importance. "Find your pack," said Albert under his breath, echoing is late father's wishes.

"Yo, Albert. You there?" Albert looked up and saw both of his friends studying him.

"Fine."

"You sure?"

"Yeah."

However, there revelry was inturrupted as a man in a black suit stepped in and walked to their table. "Which of you is Albert Joshua Lupine?"

"That would be me." Stood and turned to the man.

"Please come with me. Lady Strife wishes to speak with you."

Albert followed the man, making sure to keep his sword hidden in the cloak he had not removed yet. The man led him to a small air craft that was waiting outside to take him to the hospital that Victoria was still at.

The tiny airship touched down on the roof of the hospital. Although he had been present that Stife was wounded, he was somehow shocked that they had brought him here. He stepped out, two Secret Service agents on either side of him. They showed him the door to the stair well. They descended several floors before they entered a hallway and brought him to the third room on the left. As he entered, he saw the woman in a hospital gown in a bed. Next to her was another Agent who Albert recognised as Jon, his friend from the army. Jon curled his lips in such a way that his scorn was obvious. Had Albert's hood been down and his face visible, his old friend would not make such a face. As it were, his "friend" was a biggot and Albert was no longer certain that he wanted to stay friends.

"Will you excuse us?" said Ms. Strife to the agents. "Not you Agent Michaels. You may stay." Jon reaction made him seem annoyed; obviously he didn't like being in the same room with him. "I believe the two of you know each other."

"I don't know any of these devil worshippers," stated Jon. Albert was now glad he hadn't told Jon about his faith.

"Oh, really. Maybe if he weren't wearing a hood..." Victoria gestured and Albert pushed his hood back with his right hand, revealling his face. Jon's breath caught in his throat. Albert glanced at him and saw his eyes were wide open.

"I..." Jon began, but his voice trailed off.

"Do you know what Espers are?" interrupted Victoria, directing this question at Albert.

"To an extent."

"Then you know that ZERO and the Terra girl are both part Esper?" she continued.

"I do."

"Do you know why I sent forces to their village?"

"You desired their power," Albert stated with certainty.

"No." This denial confused Albert. After a while she continued, "I had reason to believe that Terra intended to raise an army against humanity." Now it was her turn to be confused as Albert chuckled. "Is something funny?"

"There is no reasonable justification for them to go to war. What could they possibly hope to gain?" retorted Albert. "Your telling me that you started a war because you were afraid they were going to attack first? Maybe this will put things in perspective: 100 years ago, my own people were murdered by people as 'holy' as him," he gestured at Jon, who moved uncomfortably, "because they were terrified that we were trying to awaken some demon and let loose his wrath. So excuse me if I don't fully trust your words."

"But they are dangerous. How was that ZERO able to trick so many people into fighting for him?"

"They weren't 'tricked.' They all joined willingly. Most had already felt entitled to the wealth and authority held by the upper classes. They all had their own greed to satisfy."
