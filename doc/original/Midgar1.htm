The radio was placed back on his belt and he turned to Roland saying "C'mon, lets go." and the two hurried towards the explossion.

They quickly hid behind a steel box for cover.

"They're just kids. How did they pull off something like this," Roland said, eyebrows furled. Albert motioned for silence as he produced a small mirror and a grenade from his pockets. He reached for a pipe and when he had it he struck the ground with it. "Well, that got their attention," commented Roland, sarcatically. Albert ignored him as he used the mirror to peer around the box. As he had hoped, the swordsman approached the sound as the gunner held back to cover him. Albert pulled the pin on his grenade but instead of throwing it, he held it for a few seconds. "What the hell are you doing! Throw it!"

"Why in the Hell didn't you tell me what you were doing back their. I almost shit myself you know." Roland stood and was visibly shaken.

"Shut up and take this guy. He's a prisoner." At about that time a guard ran in. "Actually you carry this guy," he said to the newcomer, "You take his sword," he ordered to Roland. Both complied with their charges. Albert stayed behind to survey. When he looked around he saw a figure in black in a nearby alee. "Hey you," Albert said walking towards him, but the figure turned and ran. "Hey," the soldier shouted, chasing after him. In only a few moments Albert was able to overtake this Returner. They both had their swords drawn, Albert with his held like wings and the other with a long sword held in both hands. The swords came together with a loud clang. They both parried and swung, neither able to hit the other.

"ZERO, run. We'll cover you." Two Returners arrived; they both carried guns and pointed them at Albert. Then ZERO, given time, broke away from the battle and took off.

"Dammit. That was the leader?" Albert cursed at himself.

Without thinking he stood and roared a war cry. A crimson-black void surged outward and as the energy came in contact with the Gunners, they were thrown away, their bodies now lifeless. Albert knew he had over exerted himself. He slumped over, breathing hard. Gradually his vision blurred as he fell unconsious.

When he came to he was in a bed. Looking at the ceiling, he realized he was in a hospital. He sat up and turned his head to see Lefty, Jon, and Sarah looking at him, all ecstatic to see him awake. In an attempt to be funny Albert asked, "Did I fall asleep?"

"For a little while," responded Jon.

Sarah, unable to contain herself any longer hugged him around the neck. "I'm so glad your alright." Albert stifled a moan. He didn't want to ruin the moment with pain.
