It had been no more than fifteen minutes after Rose's departure, and already Alekssander had left. After returning his belongings to his workshop, he made his way over to the Midgar library, where he currently sat, poring over old tomes. It was a favorite pastime of his, as he always used to read his grandfather's old tomes when he was a child, but now, he was doing it to help abate his anger.

He kind of felt guilty for yelling at Rose, and for causing the disbanding of the Returners, but on the other hand, he thought that it was high time to stand up for himself.

He was deep in thought, not really noticing what he was reading anymore, when a single word from the dusty old tome caught his eye:

"Magicite".

Alekssander's eyes opened wide at the word. He remembered hearing it somewhere before. The Returners... Is this what ZERO was after all this time? Is this why we attacked those reactors? He though back to when him and Rose attacked Mana Reactor B, and the creatures they saw there defending it. "Were they..."

He dived deeper into the book, determined to get answers. It wasn't long until he found them.

"Espers! They were Espers! So that's what ZERO was after!" He read a bit further, and all became clear to him.

"These Espers, they hold great magical power...but only when turned to Magicite! So, ZERO was after power? Or was he after something else?"

Suddenly, as if making a great realization, Alekssander dropped all thoughts of ZERO and looked up from the book. A blank stare stretched across his face. He had a plan, or so he thought. "Would it be wrong to get revenge? Maybe...but still. It wouldn't hurt to have some Magicite."

"...And for that, I'll need an Esper..." Frantically he returned to the book, eager to find one that would suit his needs. After a few minutes, he found the one. "And it's located here! In Midgar!" Chuckling, Alekssander shut the book and exited the library. He rushed back to his workshop to get prepared.

Both jeeps stopped for a short moment. If they moved too fast, they would not be ready for a counter attack. They left the jeeps, but both gunners remained on their turrets to provide any cover fire. While they were planning, the Wounded man peered over the crest of the hill, shielding his eyes from the sun, which was odd because the sun was behind them. The soldiers spotted him and fired, but he got his head back down quickly and avoided another injury.

The four foot soldiers began advancing again, crouching, and not daring to move to far ahead of the unit. The men on the turrets scanned the hill to make sure no one got of a pot shot. Out of the corner of his eye one of these gunners saw something strange. The old man had gotten behind the other turret gunner and had drawn one of his blades. With one motion, he decapitated him and took control of the turret. Before the old man could get a shot off, though, he was nocked off of the jeep by the force of the bullets fired from the other turret. His body fell limply on the ground. The gunner turned his attention back to the hill and was dumbstruck by the sight that greeted him: His allies had been quickly dispatched by the fugitives. How? Before he had another thought, though he openned fire at the group. One by one the were driven to the ground, even the man-of-metal quickly succumbed.

How could wounded man get up so quickly? This and many other thoughts raced through his head. He stepped down from the turret and pulled a hand gun from its holster and stepped forward to check that they were really dead. Before he could take a few steps forward though, a katana tore though his back. He collapsed under his own weight, but before he died, he saw his error: where he had gunned down his targets, only his allies lay, each and every one riddled with bullet wounds.
