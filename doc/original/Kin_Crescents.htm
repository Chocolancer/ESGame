        < User:Kinneth

        (Okay, for all purposes necessary, this character sheet is complete. Too tired to complete it fully...)

        490px


        Name: Kin Hartphilia Crescents

        Race: Aegyl

        Sex: Female

        Job: Runic Swordsman

        Nationality: Rebel

        Age: 24 years of age

        Height: 5'4, 163 cm

        Weight: 119 lbs, 54 kg

        Build: Well-toned, yet slim.


        Appearance: Kin stands 5'4 weighing in at 119 pounds. Her light brown hair falls to her neckline. She sports a pair of crimson red eyes. The ruby pupils absorb the environment, taking in the details of her surroundings with a curious flair. Her skin is unmarred and still youthful, withheld in a pristine state from the positive energy emanating from her protection runes.

        Kin's clothing do not hold much value in the realms of protection. Instead, Kin imbues her outfit, and her body itself, with protective runes. The runes both blunt physical attacks and dispels magic based attacks. The runes are not infinite in power, but they do help vastly.

        Born as an Aegyl, Kin holds the power of flight through her wings. In their full state, the wings billow out like a pair of pure angel's wings. However in most cases, Kin keeps her wings in a mini-fied state to make herself less conspicuous.


        Personality: Kin can act cool headed in the face of danger. She can act calmly in almost any situation, which allows her to strategically analyze battles with ease. Kin is not one to rush, instead opting for a more tactical outlook. However, this does not mean that she will run away from battles that are severely against her favor. As long as there is a percentage of a chance that she can win, she will try to achieve victory. Kin also thinks highly of her comrades and friends, keeping them close to her heart. She would go out of her way to help them in any situation, even at the cost of injury to her own self. However, she has no remorse for people that she does not know.


        Weapon: Her own personal sword, Ixistence. By itself, it is a strong blade; nothing special or godly, but a good blade nevertheless. She can add to the sword's power by imbuing it with runes.


        Spells: Kin holds basic knowledge of practically all elemental spells. However, she has a special affinity towards fire and ice based magic. Of course, she prefers runes over magic. Her runes hold an almost infinite amount of effects, ranging from buffs, auras, debuffs, and other uses. She also has the ability to use the mini spell, although she really only uses it to change the size of her wings. The reduction of her wing size also has the side effect of producing a lesser amount of stress on her body. Therefore, as long as she keeps them in a minimized state for the majority of the time, her life span is longer than the average Aegyl.


        Limit: Rune - Fury

        Kin's limit break is a conscious ability which she can use whenever she wants, whether she's in a fatal position or not. However, she takes care not to use it copiously, as it drains her energy at an alarmingly fast rate.

        Fury sends Kin's psyche into a berserk state. Practically all of her abilities are pushed to new heights. Her reflexes become close to instantaneous. An aura envelops her body which super heats the atmosphere that touches her skin, causing blistering burns to anyone who stays in the aura for too long (effective range is about half a foot). Her strength, speed, magic power, and defenses become stronger in this state.

        Of course, with great power comes consequences. Kin can only stay sane in this state for a brief amount of time. If she stays under the effects of Fury for too long, her body and mind starts to deteriorate. Not only that, but Fury also tires her out immensely. After she exits the berserk state, a wave of fatigue washes over the brown haired female, rendering her skills dramatically reduced. She also becomes ever more unstable after every use of the rune.


        Story: Kin was born in the magic inundated island of Lemurés. Growing up, she never really liked how cumbersome her wings were. Sure they were convenient in that they allowed her to fly. Despite that, she didn't want her wings out in their full form when she wasn't flying. From this dislike, Kin started learning her first spells. Of course, the very first spell that she worked on was the size altering spell, Mini. It was easy enough, considering the fact that she was naturally attuned to magic and the copious amounts of raw magic in the air helped. She quickly learned the simple trick and went on with shrinking her wings. From this, Kin found a peculiar side effect. Before, she thought that the physical state that she was in was normal. However, when her wings became smaller, she felt like a weight was lifted from her chest. She felt invigorated. Apparently, the reduction of the wings also reduced the stress they placed on the Aegyl's body.

        Of course, all the other kid Aegyl made fun of her wimpy wing size. She countered with the fact that since her wings were subdued, she would most likely live longer than any of them. That shut them up :)

        Anyways, she continued with her life. The belittling from the other kids trickled to a stop as they learned to accept the "eccentric" one. One day while they were out gathering Damson berries, a quake erupted from underneath their feet. Suddenly, a fissure opened up right below them due to the shaking. The other Aegyl children were safe from the crack due to their ability to fly. But Kin had her wings mini-fied when the quake hit. She slipped, plummeting down the hole in the ground. The fissure went straight through the entire length of the island, opening up on the bottom. Kin fell out right out of the bottom of the floating island. Desperately, she tried to reverse the effects of the Mini spell, but she found that it was harder to cast magic in the air outside. The presence of magic was weaker outside of Lemurés, drastically weakening her spell power. Her panicking also contributed to her inability to decast the spell. She fell right through the blanket of clouds, zooming right through them like a knife through warm butter. Now she could see the ground below. It seemed to go on forever, spanning infinitely in all directions. Back on Lemurés, there were set boundaries where the floor ended and the emptiness of the air began. But below her, that vast land, it seemed to go on with no limit. The enormous panorama stunned the young Aegyl girl momentarily. Only when she could make out the topographic details did she snap out of it. Panic started clutching at her again. She was getting closer and closer to the ground by the second. A string of incantations egressed her lips as she hysterically tried to remove the Mini spell from her wings.

        Finally, finally, it worked. Her wings spread out in their gallant fullness. She flapped them once, twice, three times - her body crashed into the rough terrain of the mountain. She was too late to slow her descent fully, only managing to send out those three flaps to slow her fall. Kin's frail twelve year old body crumpled from the impact. She blinked in and out of consciousness, traumatically weakened from the fall. She drifted off into the black fog of unconsciousness, her wings drooping over her puny body.

        Two days later in a nearby village, a ragged adolescent with great big wings stumbled in from the north entrance. Her white wings, and her body alike, were splotched with blood, presumably her own. Several villagers helped her to her feet, dragging her to an infirmary. She stayed there under the hospitality of the villagers for a fortnight. She recuperated and set out to venture the new lands ahead of her.

        Eight years. One can travel a lot in eight years with the right contacts. Of course, Kin didn't have access to these contacts, considering that she had been in an enclosed floating island all her life. All she could do was familiarize herself with these foreign lands. She gathered information in those eight years. She also learned the wonders of runes. At the end of the seventh year, she was positive that she knew where exactly she was. On the eighth, she decided to use that knowledge to accomplish something. With her trusty sword, Ixistence, she ventured out to find one of the fabled Dimensional Cracks. The Dimensional Crack would enable her to somehow "slip through the dimensions to travel enormous distances with ease."

        She arrived at the site of the nearest Dimensional Crack only to see that all of the defensive mechanisms were disabled. Puzzled, she traveled within the grotto only to bear witness to another individual standing right in front of the artifact. Damn, was she too late? All this effort to get here dashed away because someone else got here first. Wait...no. If she defeated the other one, she could take it from him. The other one didn't look so tough, cunning and clever to get past the defenses, but not tough at all. He also seemed preoccupied with the artifact in front of him, so he didn't notice Kin who had arrived behind him. She silently ran up to the man's backside, piercing the vulnerable flesh with her blade. A sharp exclamation escaped the person's throat as he uttered out in surprise. His grip on the Dimensional Crack slackened, the artifact dropping to the stone floor. A hairline fracture spindled its way across the cubed object. Kin pulled out her sword from the by now dead man, his body slumping onto the cold floor. His blood pooled out beneath him, staining his traveler's clothes with its crimson color. The Aegyl wiped down her blade, sheathing it back into its holder. She bent down, grasping the blue colored cube from the ground. A thin smile crept up on Kin's face. Success.

        (This biography was thought up impromptu so please disregard most of the shittiness. Yeah yeah, I realize that the whole explanation about the Dimensional Crack was cliched, but deal with it, I needed something to finish it up.)
