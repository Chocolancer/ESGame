A Mary-Sue is basically a character that has been made too perfect to a point that he/she becomes an "author's pet" (ex. a girl who's rich and has a ton of friends and no one hates her, she fights crime as a superhero and always wins, she's always happy and in that little world she's the center of the attention, etc.). To avoid making a "Mary Sue", try to be modest in creating your character. Try not to make him/her all too powerful, be realistic in his/her accomplishments, try to put some kind of strength/weakness factor in your character, make him/her as unique as possible. Well, that's at least my philosophies on how to avoid making one anyway...

EDIT: Don't make the character TOO unique or that will result in a Mary-Sue as well...

[edit] Mess

This forum is a mess and completely disorganised. This is how you should do it:
  • Forum:Everybody's story/Characters - Under a main heading (==,==) have "Characters", under subheadings have the armies ("Baron" and whatever you crazy kids are using), and then in further subheadings, your characters; linking with {{main}} to character's stories. Then I can see what's going on.
  • Another way to do it is to write stats. Let's say Speed, Accuracy, Strength and Magic. Give each person 50 points which they can give to each attribute. So Acc: 20, Str: 20, Speed: 5, Mag: 5. Therefore you haven't got overpowered characters.
  • You could organise a rank chart. So you have one leader of a force; then three sub-leaders; etc. This way everyone can't be the highest-ranking soldier like everyone wishes. You could also put "age-limits" on each rank to not make things ridiculous, as well as restrictions so certain ranks can't do something another can.

Just some suggestions because this page is annoying me because it is going nowhere.  ILHI 20:25, 5 March 2009 (UTC)

It can be done. I think we had 15 editors at the beginning of EoS, and it was fine (okay, it was an absolute mess. But we didn't plan it at all). If your story is similar, some people will grow bored of it by the end. Make a subpage of this forum (eg Forum:Everybody's story/Merged story), and put everything there. It's easier if you divide your characters into two or three factions, so you can write about a smaller group of the characters at once.

As for managing each of the characters...well, you have your bios, and you should develop them in your individual stories. Read all the stories until you get a feel for the characters. Then you can write how each character interacts. That's why character development (not necessarily backstory) is important: so you don't have a bunch of walking cardboard cut-outs. Discuss where the story is going on IRC. Or, have faith in your fellow editors and their ability to continue the story.

I'd like to see how the story goes once it's merged. It won't be the wiki's 4th collaborative fic until the stories all come together, ne? ^_^
